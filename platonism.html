Platonism

[From the name of the Greek philosopher Plato (427-347 BCE).]

  1. (system) The doctrines of Plato or the tradition of philosophizing that he founded. Usually the term refers to Plato's idealism and dualism, though it sometimes also refers to his collectivism, rationalism, intellectualism, distrust of art, etc.
